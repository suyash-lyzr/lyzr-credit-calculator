. The funnel multiplier is way too smallThe calculator uses 1.6× funnel (4,600 contracts → 7,360 funnel volume → 115K runs). For a regulated legal workflow at enterprise scale, the real funnel is much wider:
Contracts received but rejected at intake (wrong type, missing pages, scanned poorly): 20-30%
Contracts that need clarification rounds with the originating team: 30-40%
Contracts that go through multiple iteration rounds with counsel: 25-35%
Contracts that get re-classified after first pass: 10-15%
Test/validation runs during ramp-up: not counted at all
Exception handling (unusual contract structures): 5-10%
A realistic enterprise legal funnel is 2.5-4×, not 1.6×.2. Iteration depth per step is undercountedLook at the LLM table — most steps have "Calls/unit" between 1.0 and 3.0. For a regulated legal workflow:
Extraction runs 1-3 times (yes), but also gets re-extracted when validation fails, when counsel disputes terms, when amendments need re-processing. Real average is 4-6 calls.
Risk validation at 1.8 is way too low. Each clause that fails playbook gets re-evaluated, escalated, sometimes routed to a human, then re-checked. Real average is 3-5.
Summary drafting at 2.0 is fine for first pass but doesn't include redline iterations with counsel. Real average is 4-7.
Approval gate at 1.7 — final reviews in regulated contexts go through 2-3 reviewers, each with their own concern → fix → re-check loop. Real average is 5-8.
3. Continuous platform work isn't modelledThe calculator has zero allowance for:

Copilot conversations (every paralegal/counsel turn = 1 run, easily 50-200 turns/day across the team)
Knowledge base updates (new playbook rules, new clause patterns, regulatory changes)
Cross-contract intelligence (pattern extraction across the portfolio for negotiation leverage)
Skill tuning (continuous improvement of extraction accuracy, classifier precision)
Audit trail and compliance reporting (regulated workflows require this constantly)
This alone is typically 15-25% of total runs at enterprise scale.4. Year 1 onboarding isn't modelledFor a regulated legal workflow, Year 1 includes:

Playbook ingestion and rule encoding (~5K-10K runs)
Past contract corpus indexing (3+ years of contracts = 50K-100K runs)
Clause library training (~20K-30K runs)
Counsel-specific tone calibration (~10K-15K runs)
UAT cycles with the legal team (~30K-50K runs)
That's 100K-200K one-time runs not captured anywhere.5. The base assumption is wrongThe calculator assumes the customer told us their full volume. But "50 contracts/week" is almost certainly:

Just one contract type (NDAs only, or MSAs only)
Just one team or geography
Just the ones that reach a specific stage
Real enterprise legal volume across all contract types, all teams, all stages is typically 3-10× what gets stated initially. The calculator should challenge this number, not accept it.6. The buffer is too thin30% buffer is fine for a stable steady-state workflow.
